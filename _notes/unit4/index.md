## On-Policy vs Off-Policy Learning

- **On-policy:** the policy used to generate data is the same as the policy being improved (e.g., SARSA).
- **Off-policy:** data is generated by a **behaviour policy** $b$; a different **target policy** $\pi$ is improved (e.g., Q-learning).

Off-policy learning requires **importance sampling** in general, though Q-learning sidesteps this with a max operator.

---

## SARSA (On-Policy TD Control)

Update uses the quintuple $(S_t, A_t, R_{t+1}, S_{t+1}, A_{t+1})$:

$$q(S_t, A_t) \leftarrow q(S_t, A_t) + \alpha\bigl[R_{t+1} + \gamma\, q(S_{t+1}, A_{t+1}) - q(S_t, A_t)\bigr]$$

Policy is $\varepsilon$-greedy over $q$. SARSA converges to $q^*$ under GLIE and Robbins–Monro step-size conditions.

---

## Q-Learning (Off-Policy TD Control)

Uses a **max** over next-state actions regardless of the action actually taken:

$$q(S_t, A_t) \leftarrow q(S_t, A_t) + \alpha\bigl[R_{t+1} + \gamma \max_{a'} q(S_{t+1}, a') - q(S_t, A_t)\bigr]$$

Q-learning converges to $q^*$ directly, even if the behaviour policy explores.

<div class="problem">

**Worked Example — Cliff Walking.** Compare SARSA and Q-learning on the cliff-walking grid (Sutton &amp; Barto Example 6.6). SARSA (with small $\varepsilon$) learns a safer inland path; Q-learning learns the optimal (but risky) cliff-edge path but has higher online regret during learning. Explain this difference.

**Solution.** Q-learning bootstraps from $\max_{a'} q(S_{t+1}, a')$, which assumes optimal future actions — so it learns $q^*$ aggressively. SARSA bootstraps from $q(S_{t+1}, A_{t+1})$ where $A_{t+1} \sim \varepsilon\text{-greedy}$; the $\varepsilon$ probability of falling off the cliff gets incorporated into SARSA's value estimates, leading it to prefer safer states.

</div>

## Expected SARSA

Replaces $q(S_{t+1}, A_{t+1})$ with the expected value under the current policy:

$$q(S_t, A_t) \leftarrow q(S_t, A_t) + \alpha\Bigl[R_{t+1} + \gamma \sum_{a'} \pi(a' \mid S_{t+1})\, q(S_{t+1}, a') - q(S_t, A_t)\Bigr]$$

Lower variance than SARSA; reduces to Q-learning when the target policy is greedy.

---

## Policy Gradient Methods

Value-based methods represent $\pi$ implicitly via $q$. **Policy gradient** methods directly parameterise $\pi_\theta(a \mid s)$ and optimise $J(\theta) = v_{\pi_\theta}(s_0)$.

### Policy Gradient Theorem

$$\nabla_\theta J(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{\pi_\theta}\!\left[q_{\pi_\theta}(S, A)\, \nabla_\theta \ln \pi_\theta(A \mid S)\right]$$

The term $\nabla_\theta \ln \pi_\theta(A \mid S)$ is the **score function** — it tells us how to adjust $\theta$ to make action $A$ more (or less) likely.

---

## REINFORCE Algorithm

Monte Carlo policy gradient — uses the full return $G_t$ as an unbiased estimate of $q_{\pi_\theta}(S_t, A_t)$:

$$\theta \leftarrow \theta + \alpha\, G_t\, \nabla_\theta \ln \pi_\theta(A_t \mid S_t)$$

High variance because $G_t$ depends on the entire episode.  A **baseline** $b(s)$ (often $v_\theta(s)$) reduces variance without adding bias:

$$\theta \leftarrow \theta + \alpha\, \bigl(G_t - b(S_t)\bigr)\, \nabla_\theta \ln \pi_\theta(A_t \mid S_t)$$

<div class="problem">

**Worked Example.** Policy $\pi_\theta(a=1 \mid s) = \sigma(\theta)$ (sigmoid). One episode: $s_0 \to s_1 \to \text{terminal}$ with rewards $0, 1$, actions $1, 0$. $\theta = 0$, $\alpha = 0.1$, $\gamma = 1$.

Apply one REINFORCE update.

**Solution.** $\pi_\theta(1 \mid s_0) = \sigma(0) = 0.5$, $G_0 = 1$.

$\nabla_\theta \ln \pi_\theta(1 \mid s_0) = \nabla_\theta \ln \sigma(\theta) = 1 - \sigma(\theta) = 0.5$.

$\theta \leftarrow 0 + 0.1 \cdot 1 \cdot 0.5 = 0.05$.

</div>

---

## Actor-Critic Methods

Split the agent into two components:

- **Actor:** policy $\pi_\theta(a \mid s)$ — decides actions.
- **Critic:** value function $v_w(s)$ — evaluates the actor.

TD error $\delta_t = R_{t+1} + \gamma v_w(S_{t+1}) - v_w(S_t)$ serves as a low-variance advantage estimate:

$$\theta \leftarrow \theta + \alpha_\theta\, \delta_t\, \nabla_\theta \ln \pi_\theta(A_t \mid S_t)$$
$$w \leftarrow w + \alpha_w\, \delta_t\, \nabla_w v_w(S_t)$$

Actor-Critic combines the online nature of TD with the policy optimisation of REINFORCE.
