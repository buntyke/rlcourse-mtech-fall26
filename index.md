Welcome to **Reinforcement Learning (25PE1AS14)**, a Professional Elective for M.Tech. II Semester (AI & Data Science) at VNR VJIET.

This course introduces the mathematical foundations and algorithms of Reinforcement Learning — from multi-armed bandits and Markov Decision Processes to temporal-difference learning, policy gradient methods, and function approximation.

**Class schedule:** Monday, Thursday, Saturday (3 sessions/week — 2 theory + 1 tutorial)

**Course Objectives:**
1. Understand the fundamentals of Reinforcement Learning.
2. Learn statistical learning techniques for sequential decision-making.
3. Develop methods for creating intelligent agents.
4. Apply RL concepts to real-world problems and case studies.

**Primary Textbook:** Richard S. Sutton and Andrew G. Barto, *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction*, 2nd Ed., MIT Press.
The book is freely available at [incompleteideas.net/book/the-book-2nd.html](http://incompleteideas.net/book/the-book-2nd.html).
